Biography

I am a third year PhD student at the University of Utah, affilitated to the School of Computing and the Utah Robotics Center. I am advised by Dr. Tucker Hermans at the LL4MA lab. My current research focuses on exploring physics based planning for dexterous manipulation of novel objects and how learning can improve them (Project Page).

I graduated with a Bachelors in Mechatronics from SASTRA University. My undergraduate thesis was on generating odometry for a mobile robot from Laser scan data(URL).

Selected Publications

Geometric In-Hand Regrasp Planning: Alternating Optimization of Finger Gaits and In-Grasp Manipulation

This paper explores the problem of autonomous, in-hand regrasping–the problem of moving from an initial grasp on an object to a desired grasp using the dexterity of a robot’s fingers. We propose a planner for this problem which alternates between finger gaiting, and in-grasp manipulation.
Balakumar Sundaralingam, Tucker Hermans
In ICRA, 2018.
PDF Project Video Website

Relaxed-Rigidity Constraints: In-Grasp Manipulation using Purely Kinematic Trajectory Optimization

This paper proposes a novel approach to perform in-grasp manipulation using kinematic trajectory optimization which requires no knowledge of dynamic properties of the object or the robot. We define a cost function that attempts to maintain the initial grasp points, while relaxing the constraint that the contacts between finger and object remain rigid.
Balakumar Sundaralingam, Tucker Hermans
In RSS, 2017.
