Balakumar Sundaralingam

PhD Candidate

Utah Robotics Center

I am a Ph.D. candidate at the University of Utah, affiliated to the School of Computing and the Utah Robotics Center, advised by Prof. Tucker Hermans. My current research focuses on enabling robots equipped with dexterous hands to perform manipulation of novel objects (Project Page). My research spans across motion planning via gradient-based trajectory optimization, improving robot perception with learned tactile models, and structured inference for joint parameter & state estimation.

In summer of 2018, I interned in the Seattle Robotics Lab at NVIDIA Research, mentored by Prof. Dieter Fox. I worked with Nathan Ratliff, Ankur Handa, Stan Birchfield, and Byron Boots on projects related to improving robot tactile perception (Project Page). My primary research which explored learning a model that maps BioTac signals to force (Paper) was a finalist for the Best Manipulation Paper award at ICRA 2019!

Previously, I have worked on mobile robots, specifically on mapping with LIDAR and implemented several reactive collision avoidance methods. I have over 7 years of experience building robotic systems ranging from mobile robots to arm-hand manipulation systems. When not with robots, I enjoy baking, photographing nature and running.

I am currently on the job market, looking for research scientist positions. Email-me for an updated CV.
